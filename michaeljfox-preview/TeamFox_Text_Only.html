Next week, one of Central Virginia's tastiest traditions returns to Charlottesville as students at the University of Virginia (UVa) host the 10th Annual Pancakes for Parkinson's fundraiser. Founded in 2004, Pancakes for Parkinson's is a free pancake breakfast open to the public where guests can enjoy a delicious homemade breakfast and entertainment by UVa's famed a capella groups, learn more about Parkinson's disease and support The Michael J. Fox Foundation. Since inception, an average of 6,000 diners flock to UVa's main lawn each year — raising almost $200,000 for Parkinson's research. This year, the event will be held on Saturday, October 26 from 9-2 p.m.

Don’t forget, there are lots of fun seasonal activities in the greater Charlottesville area to round out your day or weekend trip: enjoy the beautiful Shenandoah Valley and famous Skyline Drive during peak foliage; visit Thomas Jefferson’s historic Monticello home; pick apples at Carter Mountain Orchard or make a few stops along the Monticello Wine Trail. The possibilities are endless!

Not able to attend, but would still like to help the UVa Pancakes for Parkinson’s team cross the $200,000-mark? Visit their event site to learn more and make an online donation.

All the best,

Team Fox
